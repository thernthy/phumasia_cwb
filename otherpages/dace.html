    • Khmer dance started during era of Angkor Kingdom between 9th and 15th century. But, under Porpoto administration, about 90% of traditional culture leaders were killed and their culture was almost destroyed. After the civil war, some survival dancers tried to revive their Khmer dance, and now succeed in continuing to keep their culture. Students in Phum Asia learn Khmer dance and try to perform and teach to next generation. We also provide “Performance show with dinner” for tourists. We have the dance stage in our place that was self-built in 2015 and can perform several kinds of dance like Apsara or tribe dance. You can see their dance on our YouTube.

    Mentor

    Mr. Mao Diy cotributes to preserve khmer culture, especially in Kampong Thom province. He gets youth together in Phum Asia to teach cultures.

    Recent Activities

    5 senior students of Phum Asia visited Japan. They performed Khmer dance at more than 30 places in a month and communicated with our customers in Japan.

    6 young students of Phum Asia started teaching Khmer dance in 2 communities every week. There are more than 30 kids to study Khmer dance in the communities.
